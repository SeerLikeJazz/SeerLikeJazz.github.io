Reserch on probe-type flexible MEMS brain computer interface device

Abstract

  • Developed a data acquisition system that can collect invasive brain signals in real time, satisfying the characteristics of miniaturization and integration of invasive brain-computer interface system.
  • A new type of probe-type flexible device for implantable brain-computer interface has been developed. The method and process parameters of electrode preparation were studied. The electrochemical performance and stability of the electrodes were tested.
  • simulating brain environment with PBS solution, and simulating nerve signals with high-precision electric signal generator to test the feasibility and stability of the system.

Data acquisition system

The circuit primarily consists of Intan Technologies’ 16-channel front-end electrophysiology signal acquisition chip RHD2116, with the core control unit utilizing ST’s STM32F407 microcontroller running at a clock frequency of 168 MHz. Data transmission is achieved through an Ethernet interface chip W5500 internally integrated full hardware TCP/IP protocol stack, MAC, and PHY. Communication between the hardware system and the host computer is accomplished through the UDP protocol.

Circuit Diagram

The circuit has dimensions of 4.2cm x 4.2cm and weighs 22g (including the battery), making it extremely lightweight and suitable for brain-computer interface applications and biological experiments. Experimental results indicate that the 16-channel acquisition system exhibits input noise as low as 7 μV, with a per-channel sampling frequency of up to 20 kHz and a sampling resolution of 16 bits, meeting the requirements for invasive neural signal acquisition.

Top and bottom View

Flexible electrode

The entire device is constructed using biocompatible materials, with Au (thickness 300nm) used for wires, electrode points, and pads. Parylene (thickness 4μm) serves as the substrate and insulation layer, while PEG is used as an adhesive material to attach the electrodes to the PI tube. The PI tube (outer diameter 330μm) serves as auxiliary implant support material.

The electrochemical properties of the electrodes are enhanced through the electrochemical deposition of conductive polymer PEDOT:PSS. The modified electrodes’ electrochemical stability and polymer adhesion strength were validated through 500 cycles of cyclic voltammetry testing and 10 minutes of ultrasound treatment. The electrodes’ voltammetric characteristics and electrochemical impedance spectroscopy were tested and analyzed.

Research has focused on improving the accuracy of implantation for flexible electrodes through the combination of biocompatible materials and PI tubes.

Testing

In order to verify the accuracy and functional stability of the entire system from electrode front-end to data acquisition circuit back-end, a gelatin and PBS solution was used to simulate the brain tissue environment. This was done to validate the precise implantation of flexible microelectrodes and assist in separating the PI tube and flexible electrodes within the environment. Additionally, a miniature signal generator was used to simulate the amplitude and frequency of neural signals. Online experiments were conducted in an in vitro simulated brain tissue environment to test signal acquisition, data preprocessing, data transmission to the host computer, and real-time waveform display on the host computer.
