eConAlpha:

Abstract

eCon-Alpha is a wearable electromyographic armband that utilizes the collection of forearm muscle neural electrical signals and limb movement signals for natural gesture recognition. It accurately predicts user limb movements, allowing users to intuitively control the Smart devices.

Prototype



eCon-Alpha can collect 8 channels of EMG (Electromyography) and 6 degrees of IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) signals.

In the first version, eCon-Alpha employed an MCU (stm32F411ceu6) + Bluetooth module solution, which had the issue of high power consumption and short battery life.

In the second version, eCon-Alpha utilizes the nRF52832 BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) chip with Bluetooth 5.0 protocol, significantly reducing power consumption. With a 200mAh battery, it can be used for more than 10 hours.

However, another issue arose; the effective working distance became very short. After ruling out issues with embedded code, I finally discovered it was an antenna impedance matching problem.Both boards (transmitter and receiver) need to be debugged by Network analyzer.

Application demonstration

eCon-Alpha comes with a PC software (developed by my colleague who works on software) for demonstrating gesture recognition. It allows the selection of six actions (which can be more), with each action being trained for five seconds. Then, the data is windowed, and four-dimensional time-domain features of electromyography (MAV, ZC, SSC, WL) are extracted. An LDA classifier is trained, and finally, online testing can be performed. During online testing, a sliding window processing is applied with a window length of 300ms and a step length of 100ms. The recognition accuracy for the six gesture classes can exceed 95%.
